About RIQE

The Rindler Institute of Quantitative Education (RIQE) is a selective, focused prospective nonprofit dedicated to helping students break into quantitative finance, applied mathematics, and computational research.

We run intensive, small-cohort programs where students work on real projects. Our goal is to assemble a small, highly motivated team of prospective nonprofit quantitative analysts to develop and rigorously backtest trading strategies using paper-traded portfolios.

RIQE emphasizes collaboration, mentorship, and open sharing of results. Participants build portfolios of code, research write-ups, and presentations they can use when applying to internships, graduate programs, and early-career roles.

Benefits of Joining

In addition to mentorship and project experience, RIQE participants may be considered for scholarship-style support. Students will also have structured opportunities to network with peers and invited guest speakers from quantitative finance, applied mathematics, and computational research.

The program is funded and designed to mirror the structure of an actual quantitative research lab, with resources allocated for data and collaborative project development. Participants gain experience working in a realistic research environment: building models, testing strategies, and communicating results as they would in a professional quant setting.

Program Awards & Support

RIQE aims to remove financial barriers for driven students who want to pursue quantitative research and education. A portion of our funding is reserved for scholarship-style support tied to participation and impact in the program.

While awards and amounts may vary year to year based on available funding, our goal is to recognize students who demonstrate strong technical growth, collaboration, and initiative.

Rindler Scholar Awards

All participants in the program are eligible to receive merit-based stipends, awarded in recognition of exceptional performance on RIQE projects, research, and presentations.

Data & Resources Support

Funding may be used to cover access to data, compute credits, or specialized tools needed for quantitative research and strategy development.

Conference & Outreach Support

In some cases, RIQE may help support students presenting their work at meetups, competitions, or academic/industry events aligned with the institute’s mission.

Details about program award availability for the upcoming cohort will be shared with applicants during the selection process. If funding is a concern, we strongly encourage you to mention it in your application so we can consider you for support.

Program Curriculum & Timeline

A typical RIQE cohort runs over a focused summer term and is built around three pillars: foundations in quantitative thinking, hands-on strategy development, and a capstone research project presented at the end of the program.

Participants can expect a structured weekly rhythm with a mix of short lectures, guided problem sets, strategy “lab” sessions, and independent project time. Workloads are designed to be rigorous but compatible with other summer commitments.

Weeks 1–3 — Foundations Students build shared tools and vocabulary for quantitative work: daily and log returns, basic probability and statistics, risk and performance metrics (e.g., volatility, Sharpe ratio), and time-series data handling in C++. By the end of this phase, each participant has implemented and evaluated simple benchmark strategies on historical data.
Weeks 4–6 — Strategies & Research The focus shifts to developing and stress-testing concrete ideas: trend-following and momentum, simple mean-reversion and pairs concepts, portfolio construction, and risk controls. Students work in small groups to prototype paper-traded strategies and learn how to interpret backtests critically rather than chasing the best historical performance.
Weeks 7–10 — Capstone Projects In the final phase, each team proposes, builds, and refines a capstone research project. This includes data cleaning, model design, careful evaluation under realistic assumptions, and a written research-style report. The program concludes with short talks where teams present their findings, code, and lessons learned.
Typical Timeline at a Glance
  • Pre-program (2–3 weeks before start): optional C++ and math refreshers, simple data exercises.
  • Program weeks: 1–2 live sessions per week (fully remote) plus 10–15 hours of independent and team work.
  • Throughout: regular feedback from peers and mentors, code review, and opportunities to practice communicating results.
  • Final week: project presentations, written report submission, and publication of selected work to the RIQE code and research repository.

All strategy development in the program is conducted on paper-traded or simulated portfolios using historical or live market data. The emphasis is educational: learning to think like a quantitative researcher, to question models, and to communicate results clearly and responsibly.
